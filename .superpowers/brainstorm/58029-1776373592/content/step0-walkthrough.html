What are the most promising approaches to building personal AI agent fleets?
Based on your knowledge graph, there are three main approaches emerging:

1. Graph-grounded agents - Agents that query a shared knowledge graph rather than isolated vector stores. This gives them shared context and reduces hallucination. [1] [3]

2. Council architecture - Domain-specialized agents that reason independently and cross-reference signals. Your meeting with Marcus on March 12 discussed this exact pattern. [2]

3. Continuous ingestion loops - Rather than one-shot RAG, agents that maintain evolving knowledge through scheduled ingestion. [4]
● Council Perspectives
🧠 AI Strategy 92%
Graph-grounded agents are the strongest approach. The shared context model avoids the "agent amnesia" problem I've seen across 12 sources.
📊 Product Growth 78%
For market positioning, the council architecture is most defensible. It's hard to replicate and creates compounding value.
🔗 Knowledge Systems 85%
All three approaches benefit from rich ontology. The entity extraction quality is the bottleneck, not the agent architecture.
💼 Consulting Ops 64%
Clients are asking about agent fleets but most aren't ready. Start with single-agent use cases and expand.
